Common Issues

Common PCB Faults & Error Codes

We diagnose and repair all these common circuit board issues in fitness equipment

Power Supply Failure

No power or intermittent shutdowns due to blown components or faulty low-voltage circuits.

Burnt MOSFET / IGBT

Board overheating, sparks, or treadmill stops suddenly.

Relay & Fuse Issues

Equipment won't start, clicks but no motor response.

Motor Drive Circuit Error

Error codes like E1/E2 on treadmills; belt not moving.

Console Communication Loss

Display works but doesn't send commands to motor board.

Over-temperature Sensor Fault

Unit shuts down mid-workout; fan keeps running.

Connector / Solder Cracks

Loose or damaged solder joints causing intermittent failures.

Our Process

PCB Repair Process

Our systematic approach to diagnosing and repairing your fitness equipment circuit boards

1

Initial Diagnostic

We begin with a thorough visual inspection and basic electrical testing to identify obvious issues like burnt components, bulging capacitors, or damaged traces.

2

Component-Level Testing

Using specialized equipment, we test individual components including MOSFETs, capacitors, resistors, ICs, and connectors to identify failed parts.

3

Circuit Analysis

We analyze the circuit design to understand how components interact, helping us identify not just failed parts but the reasons behind their failure.

4

Component Replacement

Using high-quality replacement parts, we carefully replace failed components with proper soldering techniques and attention to thermal management.

5

Functional Testing

After repairs, we thoroughly test the board under load conditions to ensure it performs to manufacturer specifications before returning to service.

6

Preventative Measures

Where appropriate, we implement upgrades or modifications to prevent future failures, such as improved cooling or higher-rated components.
